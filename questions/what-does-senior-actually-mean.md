# What does "Senior" actually mean, beyond the job title?

## Why this is open

My stated goal is "Senior full-stack developer by June 2028." I can name the title but I cannot yet name the *threshold*. Without a threshold, I cannot tell whether I am closer to it this month than last.

## Working hypotheses

1. **Senior = produces good outcomes in unfamiliar contexts.** A middle developer is excellent in the area they know. A senior is at least adequate everywhere and excellent somewhere.
2. **Senior = takes responsibility for systems, not tasks.** Middles own features; seniors own the consequences of those features over time (performance, maintenance, on-call).
3. **Senior = multiplies the team.** A middle ships their own work. A senior makes the people around them ship better work.

These are not exclusive — probably all three matter, weighted differently in different companies.

## What I do not yet know

- Which of these actually evaluate against? (Action: ask my manager.)
- Are there gaps in my fundamentals that will block me regardless of how senior I "behave"? (Action: a quarterly self-audit against a public seniority rubric.)

## When I'll close this

When I have written down a personal definition of Senior, with three measurable signals, and a reason to believe it is right.
